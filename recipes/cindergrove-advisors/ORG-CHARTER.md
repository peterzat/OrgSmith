Cindergrove Advisors is a five-person operations consultancy founded in
1995, serving regional manufacturers and distributors. It is a firm of
its era: proposals and memos are typed in Word and printed for the file,
engagement letters come back countersigned by fax and are scanned into
the share by the office administrator, steering-committee decks are
plain PowerPoint with bullet slides, and the annual numbers live in an
Excel workbook kept by the President. Writing is formal but unfussy
mid-90s American business prose: complete sentences, courteous closings,
no buzzwords that would postdate the document. Consultants refer to
clients by company name and to each other by full name in anything a
client might read.
