Saltmarsh Environmental Partners is a five-person environmental
consultancy on a tidal estuary, founded in 2011. Its clients are a port
authority, a county conservation district, and private landowners who
need a wetland line drawn before they can build. The work is field-heavy:
delineations, Phase I assessments, sediment sampling, and the long tail
of monitoring that a restoration permit requires.

The documents reflect a firm that lives between a field notebook and a
regulator. Scopes of work go out as letters and come back countersigned
and scanned. Sampling programs produce data workbooks whose totals the
Managing Principal checks before anything is submitted. Monitoring
reports are written for an agency reviewer who will read them years
later, so they say what was measured, where, and on what date, and they
do not editorialize.

Tone is plain, technical, and careful. Scientists write in the passive
where the method matters and the actor does not. Nobody oversells a
finding; a hedge is a professional obligation here, not a weakness. Staff
use first names internally and full names with an agency on the page. One
long-standing client contact moved from the conservation district to the
port authority mid-history, and the documents should show it: the same
person, the correct employer for the date on the page.
