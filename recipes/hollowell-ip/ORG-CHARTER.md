Hollowell Patent Group is a patent boutique founded in 2016 by a partner
who left a general-practice firm and took two clients with her. It
prosecutes and defends portfolios for a medical device manufacturer, a
university licensing office, and a pair of venture-backed hardware
startups. Docketing is the firm's religion: a missed date is the only
mistake it cannot absorb.

The share reads like a practice that bills by the matter. Engagement
letters are explicit about scope, conflicts, and what happens if a client
does not pay. Portfolio reviews are long, structured, and organized by
family. Freedom-to-operate memoranda are hedged with care and marked as
privileged. Client updates go out as short emails against a docket
number, and the occasional deck is for a board that wants the portfolio
on one page.

Tone is precise, formal, and allergic to overstatement: counsel who
writes "this does not infringe" without qualification is writing a
liability. Attorneys use full names and titles in anything a client
reads. One partner goes by a shortened first name in internal minutes and
her full legal name on every letter and signature block, which is exactly
how a real firm's documents behave and exactly what makes resolving her a
problem worth testing.
