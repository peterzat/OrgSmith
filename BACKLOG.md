### letterhead-duplicated-in-letters
- **Every rendered engagement letter prints the firm name twice at the top: once in the renderer's letterhead/running header and again as the first body line, directly above the client's inside address, so the recipient block reads as if the letter were addressed to the firm itself.** Found by the M8 board against the regenerated dev-mini (`rf:docplaus-letterhead-dup`, major, d:0001/d:0005/d:0008). Root cause is a render/DocIR interaction: the author opens each letter with a firm-name heading block, and `render/pdf.py` (and the docx letterhead path) already supplies the firm name as letterhead, so it doubles. It is corpus-wide, not dev-mini-specific: the engagement letter is the canonical PDF genre in every recipe, and every committed letter predates the finding.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (the fabric's time dimension); a rendering/authoring-convention bug, not a fabric one. Not urgent because it validates clean (no rule checks letterhead structure) and reads as a stylistic doubling rather than a wrong fact. The fix is a real decision: either the letter briefs must stop opening with a firm-name heading (an authoring-instruction change, so a brief/guidance edit) or the renderer must suppress a leading heading that duplicates the letterhead (a render change). Deciding which is the work.
- **Revisit criteria:** the M9 document-supply rewrite, which re-renders every letter and is the natural place to settle the letterhead convention; or any turn that touches `render/pdf.py` letterhead handling or the engagement_letter guidance in `authoring/contexts.py`; or the first board that reports the same doubling on a new org.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M8 board finding against the regenerated dev-mini).

### acl-blind-to-departure
- **The open-posture ACL grants a departed employee read access to every document created after they left.** Found by the M8 board (`rf:graphacl-1`, minor, dev-mini). `acl.py` builds grants over every entry in `foundation.people` and, for posture `open`, hands each person every manifest path, ignoring `employment.end` and the document date. So a departed analyst (Susan Wiley, out 2020-10-13) reads the 2021 engagement and both later financial summaries. The org models the departure as a first-class dated fact (`roster_churn.departures`), but the derived ACL overlay is time-agnostic. Correct-by-construction against today's `open` posture (ACL-01/02/03 pass; no rule references employment dates), so it is a realism gap, not a validator failure.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history), and only newly visible because M8 is what first made anyone depart mid-history: pre-churn every person spanned the whole range, so an employment-blind ACL was indistinguishable from a correct one. The fix is a real decision, not a patch: scope `open` grants to `employment` windows (and decide whether a departed person keeps access to documents from their tenure or loses it entirely), which changes the ACL model for every org, or accept it and document that `open` means "current and former staff".
- **Revisit criteria:** any turn that touches `acl.py` or adds an ACL posture; the M11 reference fleet if an org is meant to exercise realistic departed-employee access; or an eval question that turns on who could read what and when (the visibility suite), where a departed employee's grant would be a wrong answer.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M8 board finding; the churn work exposed it).

### recipe-growth-outruns-headcount
- **Behavioral finance exposes that every committed recipe's `growth_rate` is incoherent with its fixed `headcount`, and the old model was hiding it.** When `expense_total` was `revenue * expense_ratio` the realized ratio was the recipe's ratio by definition, forever. Now that compensation tracks headcount, a firm that compounds fees 11% a year with the same five people posts a rising margin, because that is what the recipe actually describes. Measured at M8 across the fleet (first full year -> last year): gladepoint-strategies 0.75 -> 0.45, dev-mini 0.78 -> 0.58, fernhollow-partners 0.71 -> 0.52, torchlake-engineering 0.85 -> 0.66; the shortest-range orgs drift least. A 55% net margin is not a professional-services firm. The model is right and the recipes are wrong: revenue per head goes from ~280K to ~530K over eight years with nobody hired.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8, which is the fabric's time dimension. Nothing here is a code defect -- `test_the_first_full_year_is_calibrated_to_expense_ratio` pins the one thing `expense_ratio` still promises, and it holds. The fix is a recipe question (match `growth_rate` to a roster that grows, or let `headcount` scale with the years) and every recipe is rewritten wholesale in M11, so fixing them now is work thrown away. Deliberately not patched by re-coupling compensation to revenue: that would restore the exact lockstep `rf:finance-1` indicts, to make an unrealistic recipe look realistic.
- **Revisit criteria:** M11, the fleet reset, where the four new recipes must be internally coherent -- a recipe's growth, headcount, and span have to describe the same firm, and this is the first thing that will show if they do not. Also any turn that adds a headcount-over-time knob (the natural fix: `headcount` as a schedule rather than a constant), or the first board finding reporting an implausible margin on a generated org.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M8; found by building the behavioral expense model and measuring the realized ratio across the fleet).

### pdf-newline-flattening
- **The PDF renderer silently flattens `\n` inside a paragraph block while the DOCX renderer honors it as `<w:br/>`.** `render/pdf.py:90` emits `<p>{text}</p>`, and HTML collapses newlines to spaces; an author using the same convention that works in DOCX gets a smeared line in PDF. Affects the committed fixture `gladepoint-strategies` `d:0008`, whose addressee block renders as one run-on line. Validates clean because no rule checks line breaks.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M7 (the quality instrument); found BY the M7 board rather than planned. Fixing it re-renders a frozen fixture's committed bytes, which is its own decision.
- **Revisit criteria:** any turn that touches `render/pdf.py`, or the first fixture regeneration that would re-render an affected PDF, or a board finding that reports the same smear on a new org. **Updated at M8 close (2026-07-16):** M8 was re-scoped to commit no eighth fixture and to regenerate only `dev-mini`, whose letters do not carry the multi-line addressee block that triggers the smear, so nothing was born with it this turn. The natural fix point is M9, which introduces the document-supply model and re-renders every letter, and where the letterhead convention is being settled anyway (see `letterhead-duplicated-in-letters`, a sibling render/authoring finding on the same genre). The freeze lift also removes the "re-renders a frozen fixture's committed bytes" objection for the v2.0 window.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (found by the review board during the model A/B; see docs/MODEL-AB.md finding 3).

### board-negative-control
- **The review board's false-positive rate is unmeasured: it has been calibrated on one org, one model, one run, with no negative control** (`docs/REVIEW-CALIBRATION.md`, and the README's "What this does not prove" section says so publicly). Nothing establishes how often `/forge-review` manufactures a finding against prose that is fine.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history and date-scoped briefs). Judged lower-urgency because every one of the M7 board's 11 majors against fernhollow cites ledger-traceable evidence and is checkable by hand without the board: `rf:finance-1`'s claim that every expense line is a fixed share of revenue is confirmed in 30 seconds by reading `fabric/finance.py:12-18,48` (hardcoded `_EXPENSE_CATEGORIES` weights, split out of an `expense_total` that is itself `year_revenue * expense_ratio`). A negative control matters when findings are not self-verifying.
- **Revisit criteria:** the board is pointed at a corpus whose ground truth the reader has not already read (a new fixture, an outside org, or the M10 reference fleet); or a board finding drives expensive work and cannot be confirmed against a ledger; or `/forge-fix` is proposed, since a fix loop turns the critic into a gate and its FP rate becomes load-bearing.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M7 proposal, "The board has no negative control").

### charter-redump-drift
- **`run_charter` writes `charter.json` unconditionally (`orgsmith/charter.py:59`), so re-deriving a committed fixture's charter from its recipe rewrites the file with inert default fields it did not carry when generated.** Measured today, re-deriving from `recipes/<slug>/ORG-CHARTER.md`: 5 of 7 fixtures drift (torchlake-engineering +8 fields including `hard_cases` and `acl_posture`, quillbrook-appraisal +7, bramblewood-legal +6, cindergrove-advisors and gladepoint-strategies +1 each); only dev-mini and fernhollow-partners are byte-identical, because they are the two regenerated most recently. `/forge <slug>` runs `orgsmith charter <slug>` unconditionally (`.claude/skills/forge/SKILL.md:44`), so the resume path the README advertises as a headline feature ("kill the session mid-generation, re-run `/forge <slug>`") dirties a frozen fixture's committed bytes. Contrast `run_scaffold`, which is immutable-once-written (`foundation/scaffold.py:327`).
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history and date-scoped briefs). Not urgent because the drift is semantically inert (every gained field equals the default that was implicit at generation), so validation still passes and no ground truth moves; the cost is a dirty git tree on a fixture the project calls frozen, not a wrong org. The fix is a real decision, not a patch: either guard the write the way scaffold does, or accept re-dump and stop calling charter.json frozen.
- **Now partly fenced, which lowers the urgency again but does not close it.** `tests/test_org_regen.py::test_committed_charter_redump_stays_additive` (added 2026-07-16) pins the invariant that survives either resolution: a re-dump may gain a key, never drop one or move a value. Re-measured at leaf level across the fleet: 23 gained, 0 lost, 0 changed, every gained value a default. So the drift can no longer turn into silent ground-truth movement without a red test; what remains open is only whether charter.json is byte-frozen like the rest of the fixture.
- **Revisit criteria:** M8 landing, which adds at least three more inert charter fields (roster churn, expense model, era naming) and would widen the drift to 11 fields on torchlake and start dev-mini and fernhollow drifting for the first time; or the first time someone resumes `/forge` on a committed fixture and has to explain the diff. **Resolving it either way means tightening that test:** guard the write, and the charter joins the byte-identical diff beside the ledgers; accept the re-dump, and the additive assertion is the permanent contract and the README/CLAUDE.md wording about frozen fixtures needs to say so.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M7 Context flagged it as still open and uncarried; M8 Context carries it forward and this entry is where it survives turn close).

### email-thread-spacing
- **A generated email "thread" is messages 45 days apart.** `docplan/planner.py:296` dates the k-th mail of an engagement at `eng.start + 30 + 45*k` and clamps to the charter range, so successive messages in the same thread land a month and a half apart and pile up at the range boundary once k grows. Real threads run hours to days. The correctness of the wrap is tested (`tests/test_unit_eml.py:26,55` plans 4 mails over 3 engagements and asserts the round-robin lands a second mail on one of them), but nothing checks that the resulting spacing is plausible, and no committed fixture exercises k>0 at all: all 4 `.eml` files in the fleet are "Email 1".
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history and date-scoped briefs). Email volume is the fleet's largest fidelity gap and is not on the roadmap through M11; the README now states this limit plainly rather than implying `format_mix.eml` is all that stands in the way. Fixing it is a realism question (what does a thread's cadence look like?) that wants a fixture to test against, and no email-dominant fixture exists.
- **Revisit criteria:** any turn that targets email realism or writes an email-dominant recipe; the M10 reference fleet, if one of its orgs is meant to be email-heavy (`docs/SCALE.md` allows one deliberate oddity per org); or the first committed recipe that raises `format_mix.eml` above roughly one per engagement, which is the point the 45-day cadence becomes visible in shipped files.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (found while verifying a README claim that email volume was only a missing fixture rather than a missing capability; the claim was wrong and was corrected before it shipped).

### org-tier-scaling-plan
- **Decide what splits the `org` tier when the fleet outgrows a single all-fixtures run.** Today it validates 7 fixtures / 107 files and re-derives all seven from their recipes in 1.51s (51 tests), still ~3x inside SPEC's ~5s budget, so no split is warranted. Regeneration is not what will break the budget: the whole fleet re-derives in ~60ms and scales with recipe count, while validation scales with file count. `docs/SCALE.md` puts the per-org ceiling near 330 files, and M10 (reference fleet) plus M11 (flagship org) would multiply both axes at once. The question to settle then: a fast subset over one representative fixture per capability, versus a full-fleet tier that stops being an every-push gate.
- **Why deferred:** premature at 1.06s; a tier split that saves nothing measurable is ceremony, and the right split depends on which capabilities the M10 fleet actually spreads across orgs.
- **Revisit criteria:** the `org` tier crossing ~10s wall (measure with `bin/test org`); or the M10 reference fleet landing; or the first committed org above ~150 files.
- **Origin:** `tester design 2026-07-16`
