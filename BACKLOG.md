### board-negative-control (ACTIVE in spec 2026-07-17)
- **The review board's false-positive rate is unmeasured: it has been calibrated on one org, one model, one run, with no negative control** (`docs/REVIEW-CALIBRATION.md`, and the README's "What this does not prove" section says so publicly). Nothing establishes how often `/forge-review` manufactures a finding against prose that is fine.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history and date-scoped briefs). Judged lower-urgency because every one of the M7 board's 11 majors against fernhollow cites ledger-traceable evidence and is checkable by hand without the board: `rf:finance-1`'s claim that every expense line is a fixed share of revenue is confirmed in 30 seconds by reading `fabric/finance.py:12-18,48` (hardcoded `_EXPENSE_CATEGORIES` weights, split out of an `expense_total` that is itself `year_revenue * expense_ratio`). A negative control matters when findings are not self-verifying.
- **Revisit criteria:** the board is pointed at a corpus whose ground truth the reader has not already read (a new fixture, an outside org, or the M10 reference fleet); or a board finding drives expensive work and cannot be confirmed against a ledger; or `/forge-fix` is proposed, since a fix loop turns the critic into a gate and its FP rate becomes load-bearing.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M7 proposal, "The board has no negative control").

- **Revisit criteria:** the next time `dev-mini` is regenerated for any other reason (an authoring-affecting change, or the M11 re-freeze if it re-authors the tracer); or the first time dev-mini's finance ledger is cited as an example of the behavioral expense model rather than used as a regression oracle; or any reader or board finding calling the margin out, since dev-mini leads the README's fleet table and is the org people browse first.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M11a; found by building the fleet coherence check and measuring every recipe against it).

### recipe-brief-leaks-genre-spec
- **A recipe's narrative brief that describes its document genres gets authored back out as firm philosophy.** Both blind boards in MODEL-AB Round 2 raised this as a major against both arms: the Firm Overview recites "Kickoff memos set out what the team will do in the first thirty days... Meeting minutes record what was decided, not what was merely discussed," which is the recipe's own brief wearing a costume. The committed `recipes/meridian-actuarial/ORG-CHARTER.md` has the same shape ("Its documents are the paper trail of a regulated opinion. Engagement letters are precise about scope, data reliance..."), so this reaches the real fleet, not just the probe. A firm's capability statement does not enumerate its internal memo genres.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M11b, which generates the fleet from recipes that are already written and coherence-checked; rewriting six briefs mid-turn would invalidate the tracer's byte pin and the measured margins for no gain the turn needs. The fix is also not obviously "delete the genre prose" -- the brief is where a recipe sets voice, and the genre descriptions are doing real work for per-document register.
- **Revisit criteria:** the M11b flagship board raises it again against a committed fleet org (it is predicted to); or the next time any fleet recipe's brief is edited for another reason; or `company_overview` is given a genre-specific brief that can carry voice without reciting the manifest.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-17 (M11b; found by MODEL-AB Round 2's blind board, raised independently against both arms).

### board-needs-scratch-isolation
- **Concurrently dispatched board reviewers share one session scratchpad and can read each other's extracted text.** In MODEL-AB Round 2, four reviewers ran at once over two orgs; one reviewer's `d00xx_*.txt` extractions appeared in another's working directory, and that reviewer then asserted a checkable falsehood about the *other* arm (claiming byte-identical prose across arms; all 22 docs differ). The reviewer noticed the stray files and said so, which is the only reason it was caught. Per-arm verdicts stayed sound because each reviewer read its own share directory.
- **Why deferred:** `/forge-review` dispatches per-slug over one org, so its six concurrent reviewers all read the same share by design and there is nothing to cross-contaminate. The defect only bites a board dispatched across two or more orgs at once, which no committed skill does today. Fixing it now is scaffolding for a caller that does not exist.
- **Revisit criteria:** any board is dispatched over more than one org in a single session (a fleet-wide board pass is the obvious candidate); or `/forge-review` gains a multi-slug mode; or a reviewer cites evidence that cannot be located in the org it was assigned.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-17 (M11b; found by MODEL-AB Round 2's board harness, not by the board's findings).
