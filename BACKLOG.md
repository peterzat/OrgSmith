### board-negative-control (ACTIVE in spec 2026-07-17)
- **The review board's false-positive rate is unmeasured: it has been calibrated on one org, one model, one run, with no negative control** (`docs/REVIEW-CALIBRATION.md`, and the README's "What this does not prove" section says so publicly). Nothing establishes how often `/forge-review` manufactures a finding against prose that is fine.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M8 (fabric history and date-scoped briefs). Judged lower-urgency because every one of the M7 board's 11 majors against fernhollow cites ledger-traceable evidence and is checkable by hand without the board: `rf:finance-1`'s claim that every expense line is a fixed share of revenue is confirmed in 30 seconds by reading `fabric/finance.py:12-18,48` (hardcoded `_EXPENSE_CATEGORIES` weights, split out of an `expense_total` that is itself `year_revenue * expense_ratio`). A negative control matters when findings are not self-verifying.
- **Revisit criteria:** the board is pointed at a corpus whose ground truth the reader has not already read (a new fixture, an outside org, or the M10 reference fleet); or a board finding drives expensive work and cannot be confirmed against a ledger; or `/forge-fix` is proposed, since a fix loop turns the critic into a gate and its FP rate becomes load-bearing.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M7 proposal, "The board has no negative control").

- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-16 (M11a; found by building the fleet coherence check and measuring every recipe against it).

### engagement-ledger-reads-as-whole-book
- **The firm-overview prose presents the sampled engagement ledger as the firm's complete book of business, contradicting the finance ledger by 20-60x.** Two of six M11b board reviewers drew this contradiction independently, on different dimensions (`finance_realism`, "contradicts the revenue on the firm's own financial summaries by roughly twenty-fold"; `narrative_consistency`, "the finance ledger contradicts by roughly fortyfold"), and a third (`org_realism`) independently reached the premise under it: the firm hires into an engagement book that is empty for years. (This entry read "four of six, and by implication document_plausibility" until 2026-07-17; a prior review BLOCKed the same miscount in the README and did not fix it here. `document_plausibility` never raised it -- its majors are the duplicate fee headings and the weekend meetings.) Verified against ledgers rather than taken on the board's word: engagement fees are **1.6% to 5.1% of lifetime revenue in every fleet org** -- northgate $500,500 of fees against $20,712,000 revenue (2.4%), meridian $366,500 against $23,004,000 (1.6%), hollowell 2.1%, verdant 2.9%, brackenridge 3.5%, saltmarsh 2.4%, dev-mini 5.1%. The ledger is a deliberate *sample* (`engagements.count` is a document-supply knob), but `firm_digest` hands the model only those engagements, so the model reasonably writes them up as the whole client list: northgate's 2021 overview says "The firm has been retained for five engagements to date... That is a deliberately short list" and "That is the whole business", while the FY2021 sheet one folder away posts $2,469,000 for that year alone. northgate also posts record revenue across 2020-2022 with zero engagements running.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M11b, and not fixable by editing prose -- `/forge-review`'s own rule is that a committed fixture's prose is never fixed from board findings, and the whole fleet would need regenerating. The real fix is a charter or brief decision (teach `firm_digest` that its engagements are a sample, or derive revenue from the engagement book, or have the brief forbid completeness claims), which is a design turn, not a patch.
- **Revisit criteria:** any recipe's `engagements.count` is changed, or `base_revenue` is made a function of the engagement book; or a reader cites the fleet's revenue as incoherent; or a future org is generated whose overview is meant to carry the firm's scale honestly.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-17 (M11b; found by the flagship board against northgate-staffing, confirmed fleet-wide by direct ledger arithmetic).

### docplan-has-no-business-day-calendar
- **`docplan` draws document dates without a business-day or holiday calendar, so documents land on weekends and public holidays at chance rates.** Found by the M11b board (document_plausibility) against `northgate-staffing` and verified directly from the manifest: **19 of 53 planned documents (36%) fall on a Saturday or Sunday**, against ~29% for a uniform draw. It is invisible for most genres, but minutes assert that a meeting happened: `d:0014` records a client working session at the client's offices on **Saturday 2016-05-28** (Memorial Day weekend) and `d:0050` records one on **Tuesday 2023-07-04**, US Independence Day, with the client's General Manager attending. Neither document remarks on it. The authors handle it well when they happen to notice (`d:0025` writes around its 2017-12-31 date: "Her arrival lands at the turn of the year, which is convenient rather than awkward"), which is luck, not a mechanism.
- **Why deferred:** out of scope for M11b, and it moves every date in every ledger, so it breaks the byte pin on all seven committed orgs and forces a full fleet regeneration -- the exact cost this turn just paid. It also needs a decision rather than a patch: a US-holiday table is era-dependent and locale-dependent, and the fleet already spans 1999-2025, so "which calendar" is a real question. A cheaper partial fix is to constrain only the genres that assert attendance (meeting_minutes, engagement_email).
- **Revisit criteria:** the fleet is regenerated for any other reason; or a recipe is written for a non-US locale, where the holiday question must be answered anyway; or a reader or board cites a weekend meeting as a tell (it has now been cited once).
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-17 (M11b; found by the flagship board against northgate-staffing, confirmed by counting the manifest).

### concurrent-workers-share-one-scratchpad
- **Concurrently dispatched workers share one session scratchpad and can clobber each other's scratch files.** Affects both fan-out paths. Found first in MODEL-AB Round 2, where four board reviewers ran at once and one read another's extracted text, then asserted a checkable falsehood about the *other* arm (claiming byte-identical prose across arms; all 22 docs differed). Then observed on the live `/forge` path during the M11b fleet run: two concurrent forge-author workers overwrote each other's validation scripts, and one reported the other's batch failures under its own command. No bad artifact resulted either time -- the airlock accepted every batch, and per-arm review verdicts stayed sound because each reviewer read its own share -- but a worker that distrusts its own checks burns a turn, and a reviewer that reads a sibling's evidence can publish a false finding.
- **Why deferred:** `/forge` now tells each worker to namespace its scratch by work order (Step 3b), which held for the remaining four orgs of the fleet run, so the live path has a working mitigation. The real fix is harness-level per-worker scratch isolation, which OrgSmith does not control: the skill can only ask. `/forge-review` is per-slug over one org, so its six reviewers read the same share by design and have nothing to cross-contaminate; the reviewer half only bites a board dispatched across two or more orgs at once, which no committed skill does.
- **Revisit criteria:** the harness gains per-subagent scratch isolation (delete this then, and drop the prompt guidance from `/forge` Step 3b); or a board is dispatched over more than one org in a single session; or the prompt-level mitigation is observed failing -- a worker citing evidence that cannot be located in its own assigned batch.
- **Origin:** spec 2026-07-17 (M11b; reviewer half found by MODEL-AB Round 2's harness, author half found live during the fleet run).
